*Mohanlal Viswanathan* (born 21 May 1960), known mononymously
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mononymous_person> as *Mohanlal*, is an
Indian actor <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Actor>, producer
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Film_producer> and playback singer
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Playback_singer> who predominantly
works in Malayalam
cinema <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malayalam_cinema>. He has had a
prolific career spanning four decades, during which he has acted in more
than 300 films <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mohanlal_filmography>. In
addition to Malayalam, he has also appeared in other regional Indian films
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cinema_of_India>.[2]
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mohanlal#cite_note-2>

Mohanlal made his acting debut as a teenager in the Malayalam film *Thiranottam
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thiranottam>* in 1978, but the film was
delayed in its release for 25 years due to censorship issues. His screen
debut was in the 1980 romance film *Manjil Virinja Pookkal
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manjil_Virinja_Pookkal>*, in which he played
the villain.[3]
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mohanlal#cite_note-thiranottam-3>[4]
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mohanlal#cite_note-ManjilVirinjaPookkal-4>In
the following years, he played antagonistic characters in several films and
gradually rose to supporting roles. Towards the mid-1980s, he established
himself as a leading actor and gained stardom after starring in a series of
commercially successful films in 1986; the crime
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crime_film> drama
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drama_(film_and_television)> *Rajavinte
Makan <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rajavinte_Makan>*, released in that
year heightened his stardom. Mohanlal prefers to work in Malayalam films,
but he has also appeared in some Hindi
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bollywood>, Tamil
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tamil_cinema>, Telugu
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telugu_cinema> and Kannada
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kannada_cinema> films. Some of his best
known non-Malayalam films include the Tamil political drama *Iruvar
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iruvar>* (1997), the Hindi crime drama *Company
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Company_(film)>* (2002) and the Telugu
film *Janatha
Garage <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Janatha_Garage>* (2016).[5]
